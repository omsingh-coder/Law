Indian Family Law - Key Concepts and Sections

1. What is Family Law?

Family law in India governs personal relationships such as marriage, divorce, adoption, guardianship, succession, and maintenance. It varies according to the religion of individuals: Hindu, Muslim, Christian, and others.

2. Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

This Act governs marriage between two Hindus. Key sections include:

  • Section 5: Conditions for a valid Hindu marriage
  • Section 7: Ceremonies of Hindu marriage
  • Section 9: Restitution of conjugal rights
  • Section 13: Grounds for divorce
  • Section 13B: Divorce by mutual consent

3. Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, 1956

Governs adoption and maintenance among Hindus.
Important Sections:

  • Section 6: Requisites for valid adoption
  • Section 7 & 8: Who may adopt?
  • Section 9: Who may give in adoption?
  • Section 11: Conditions for valid adoption
  • Section 18: Maintenance of wife

4. Hindu Succession Act, 1956

This act deals with intestate succession among Hindus.

  • Section 6: Devolution of interest in coparcenary property
  • Section 8: General rules of succession in case of males
  • Section 15: General rules of succession in case of females

5. Muslim Personal Law

Based on Shariat, applies to Muslims for marriage, divorce, inheritance, etc.

  • Marriage: Nikah (contractual)
  • Dissolution: Talaq, Khula, Mubarat
  • Inheritance: Governed by Quranic shares

6. Christian Marriage Act, 1872

Applies to Christians in India. Key Provisions:

  • Notice of intended marriage
  • Marriage registration and solemnization

7. Special Marriage Act, 1954

Provides for civil marriages across religions.

  • Section 4: Conditions relating to solemnization
  • Section 5: Notice of intended marriage
  • Section 11: Objection to marriage
  • Section 27: Divorce

8. Guardians and Wards Act, 1890

Applies to appointment and powers of guardians over minors. Important in both Hindu and Muslim law for custody matters.

9. Domestic Violence Act, 2005

Protects women from domestic violence and provides legal remedies like protection order, residence order, monetary relief, etc.

10. Maintenance under Section 125 CrPC

Provides for maintenance of wife, children, and parents regardless of religion, under the Criminal Procedure Code.
