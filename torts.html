Important Concepts from Law of Torts

1. What is a Tort?

A tort is a civil wrong, other than breach of contract, which causes harm or loss and gives rise to legal liability. The person who commits a tort is called a tortfeasor.

2. Essentials of Tort

- A wrongful act or omission
- Legal damage to a person
- The wrongful act must be of such a nature that it gives rise to a legal remedy.

3. Types of Torts

- Negligence
- Nuisance
- Trespass (to person or property)
- Defamation
- Strict Liability
- Absolute Liability

4. Negligence

Negligence means failure to take proper care which a reasonable person would take. To prove negligence, three things are needed: duty of care, breach of that duty, and resulting damage.

5. Nuisance

Nuisance is an act which unlawfully interferes with a person's use or enjoyment of land. It may be public or private nuisance.

6. Trespass

It means direct interference with the person or property of another without lawful justification. It may be trespass to person (assault, battery, false imprisonment) or to property.

7. Defamation

Defamation means injury to the reputation of a person. It is of two kinds: Libel (written) and Slander (spoken).

8. Strict Liability

A person who uses dangerous substances and allows it to escape is held liable even without negligence — as per the rule in Rylands v. Fletcher.

9. Absolute Liability

This is stricter than strict liability — no exceptions allowed. Established in the Indian case of M.C. Mehta v. Union of India.

10. Vicarious Liability

One person is held responsible for the acts of another, e.g. employer for employee's torts.

11. Remedies in Tort

- Damages (Compensation)
- Injunction (order to stop doing something)
- Specific restitution of property
